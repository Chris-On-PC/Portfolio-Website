Resistive Microfluidic Device for Pathogen Detection

For my undergraduate thesis I got given the task to investiagte the possibilities of using resisitve sensing for pathogen detection. As part of the research project I designed a small circuit to supply a constant potential to a specially designed microfiber-based resistor. The signal obtained from exposing the specially engineered transducers to a biomolecule under investigation was then relayed and analyzed to enable detection.

Autophagy Flux Biosensor

During my masters degree I developed a mobile sensor for protein detection. This required extensive embedded hardware and software design. The sensor made use of intelligently designed transducers being supplied of very low current. The signal obtained from the various transducers investigated when exposed to the bioelements of interest were then filtered, and using a high-precision ADC sent to a microcontroller. The sensor was manufactured to fit on a small PCB and was connected to a transducer holder where the aqueous testing samples could be added. Via a USB connection the measurements were then relayed to Python-based computer graphical user interface. The GUI also enabled control over the sensor and all of its components.
